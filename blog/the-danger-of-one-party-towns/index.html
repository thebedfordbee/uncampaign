The Danger of One-Party Towns

May 6, 2026 ·
Facebook X / Twitter Email SMS

AI Summary

New York State has moved most local elections onto the crowded November even-year ballot, burying town races beneath national and state contests. The result is lower awareness, lower accountability, and increasingly uncontested local boards — including here in Bedford.

  • When a local race is uncontested, voters lose their clearest tool for accountability.
  • One-party dominance in a small town isn't the same as consensus — it may simply mean no one showed up to offer a choice.
  • A contested election forces both candidates to explain themselves, even if the outcome is predictable.
