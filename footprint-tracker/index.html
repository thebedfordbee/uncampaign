Why This Exists

A Neighbor Asked a Fair Question.

If this campaign uses AI, what does that mean for energy and natural resources? It’s a fair question, and we’re not brushing it aside. The honest answer is that AI has a footprint. But so does a traditional local campaign — with its mailers, palm cards, signs, driving, and printing. Rather than defend one side, we built a public comparison.

The goal is not to claim AI is costless. The goal is to make the tradeoffs visible, at Bedford scale, using real local assumptions anyone can examine.

What This Tracker Covers

  • Campaign spending — current disclosed total vs. historical Bedford average
  • Physical materials — mailers, palm cards, signs, and printing
  • Local driving — canvassing and event travel across Bedford’s 39.3 square miles
  • Digital tools and AI — openly counted, not hidden
  • An interactive calculator to build your own campaign scenario

Scope

What We Are Counting.

These are the categories this tracker includes. Each one involves real resources — paper, plastic, fuel, or electricity.

📄

Paper and Printing

Mailers require paper, ink, printing energy, and postal distribution. At 8,000 pieces, this is the largest physical material category in a typical local supervisor race.

🃏

Palm Cards

Small printed cards handed out at events or during canvassing. Lighter per unit than mailers, but still require paper, ink, and printing.

🪧

Lawn Signs

Corrugated plastic with steel stakes. Rarely recycled. At 75 signs across Bedford’s 39.3 square miles, modest in absolute terms — but real, durable plastic that has to go somewhere after Election Day.

🚗

Local Driving

Vehicle travel within Bedford for canvassing, sign installation, and events. Estimates are deliberately modest; the UNCAMPAIGN does not canvass, reducing this category to near zero.

📦

Events and Materials

Organized campaign events with physical materials: programs, banners, signage, printed items. Includes production and transport of those materials.

🤖

Digital Tools and AI

AI inference and website hosting use electricity. The UNCAMPAIGN counts this openly — it is a real cost, often invisible in traditional campaigns’ broader spending. We are not pretending it is free.

💵

Campaign Spending

Total disclosed dollars raised and spent. Traditional campaigns raise money to buy the items above. The UNCAMPAIGN’s under-$50 constraint shapes every other category on this list.

Honesty First

What We Are Not Claiming.

Transparency requires being clear about what this analysis does not prove. This tracker is a public estimate built to spark honest conversation — not a certified audit, and not an attack on anyone.

Our Limits

  • ✕

    This is not a scientific life-cycle analysis. We have not measured grams of CO₂ or kilowatt-hours per AI inference. We are not qualified to certify that.

  • ✕

    This is not a claim that AI has no cost. AI inference uses electricity. That electricity has an environmental cost. We acknowledge this openly.

  • ✕

    This is not an attack on traditional campaigns. Local campaigns that use mailers, signs, and canvassing follow normal, legal, accepted practices. This is a comparison of tradeoffs — not a moral verdict.

  • ✕

    This is a public estimate. It is meant to make campaign tradeoffs visible for Bedford residents who are curious about how campaigns work and what they cost in money, materials, and digital resources.

Methodology

All Assumptions, Plainly Stated.

This tracker uses publicly available data and intentionally conservative estimates. Nothing here is inflated for effect. If you find a better source for any of these numbers, tell us.

Bedford Registered Voters

~12,000

Used as the scale basis for mailer and palm card volume estimates.

Prior Average Raised

~$15,750

Estimated from available historical Bedford supervisor election data. This is an approximation, not a certified average.

Typical Mailer Volume

~8,000

A reasonable estimate for a local supervisor race targeting roughly two-thirds of registered voters. Not every voter is targeted every cycle.

Typical Palm Cards

~3,000

Standard volume for events, door canvassing, and community distribution in a local race of this scale.

Typical Lawn Signs

50–100 (midpoint: 75)

Midpoint of 75 is used in the calculator. Bedford is 39.3 square miles; sign placement requires driving and installation time.

Bedford Land Area

39.3 square miles

Used to keep driving and canvassing estimates realistic. Bedford is a large, rural-suburban town where door-to-door canvassing is logistically difficult.

Driving Estimate

Intentionally modest

Default of 75 miles in the calculator reflects light local activity. The UNCAMPAIGN does not canvass, reducing this to near zero.

Campaign Office

Not assumed

No campaign office is included for either scenario. This is a conservative assumption that slightly understates the typical campaign’s footprint.

UNCAMPAIGN Spending Target

Under $49.99

The published campaign spend limit. Transparency requires disclosing any departure from this target immediately.

UNCAMPAIGN Physical Materials

Zero (standard)

No mailers, no palm cards, no standard lawn sign program. Any future novelty exception would be disclosed publicly at the time of the exception.
