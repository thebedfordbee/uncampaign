Compare the Footprint

Why This Exists

A Neighbor Asked a Fair Question.

If this campaign uses AI, what does that mean for energy and natural resources? It is a fair question. AI has a real footprint. But so does a traditional local campaign — with its mailers, signs, printing, and physical infrastructure. Rather than dismiss the concern, we built a public comparison.

This page is the answer. Not defensive. Not evasive. Just transparent.

Honesty First

AI has a real footprint. AI inference uses electricity. We acknowledge this openly.

Traditional campaigns have a real footprint too. Mailers, palm cards, signs, and driving all consume real resources.

This is not a scientific life-cycle analysis. It is a transparent comparison of campaign inputs — not a certified carbon audit.

This is not an attack on traditional campaigns. What they do is legal and normal. This is a comparison of tradeoffs.

This is a public estimate meant to make campaign tradeoffs visible.

How This Was Built

This Campaign Shows Its Work

The UNCAMPAIGN uses AI openly — not secretly, not as a black box. AI may help draft, organize, code, calculate, or summarize. Don Scott is responsible for final public statements, campaign positions, and decisions. The point is transparency, not pretending technology is magic or cost-free.

AI Transparency Statement

How AI Is Used in This Campaign

This campaign uses AI tools openly. Not secretly. Not as a labor-saving shortcut that goes unacknowledged. As a transparent part of how this campaign operates — and as a demonstration that small campaigns can produce high-quality civic work without large budgets.

What AI Does

AI may help draft copy, organize information, summarize public records, write code, perform calculations, and generate first versions of documents. Every public-facing output goes through human review before it is published.

What AI Does Not Do

AI does not set campaign positions. AI does not make decisions. AI does not publish anything autonomously. Don Scott reviews and is responsible for every public statement, policy position, and campaign decision.

Why This Page Exists

A Bedford neighbor asked a fair question about AI and natural resources. This tracker was built in response — not to deflect, but to answer honestly. AI uses electricity. Traditional campaigns use paper, plastic, fuel, and money. This page is a public attempt to make those tradeoffs visible.

What This Page Is Not

This is not a scientific life-cycle analysis. It is not a carbon certification. It is not a claim that AI is costless or that traditional campaigns are wasteful. It is a transparent estimate meant to spark honest conversation about how campaigns use resources.

Human Review Pledge

Every public statement on this campaign’s website was reviewed by Don Scott before publication. If you see something that appears inaccurate or generated without human review, you can ask Don about it directly through the Ask Don page.
