A civic shortcut, not a campaign speech

Town Board meetings, in minutes.

Most people cannot sit through every Bedford Town Board meeting. That should not mean they are left out of local government. This page will collect short audio recaps designed to help residents keep up in about five minutes.

About this project

Making Bedford Easier to Follow

Local government asks a lot of residents. Meetings are long. Agendas are dense. Replays are useful, but not always realistic for busy people.

Town Board in Minutes is an experiment in making Bedford easier to follow. After each meeting, we will use publicly available meeting materials and replay video to create a short audio recap. The goal is not to replace the official record. It is to help more residents know what happened, what matters, and where to look next.

What each recap covers

  • What was discussed. The topics on the agenda, plain and clear
  • What was decided. Votes, approvals, and resolutions
  • What is still open. Items deferred, debated, or unresolved
  • What to follow next. What residents may want to track going forward

These recaps are unofficial, AI-assisted, and human-reviewed. Residents should rely on the Town's official minutes, agendas, and meeting recordings for the formal record.

Production Prompt — Placeholder 
Use the public replay video for this Bedford Town Board meeting. Create a short audio overview featuring two hosts who clearly and fairly recap the meeting for residents. How this was built

This Campaign Shows Its Work

Town Board in Minutes is part of this campaign's open use of low-cost technology. The recaps use publicly available Town Board meeting materials. AI helps turn long meetings into short, accessible audio summaries, and human review remains part of the process.

1

Public meeting materials

Each recap is built from publicly available sources: agendas, official minutes, and the Town's own meeting replay video. No private information is used.

2

AI-assisted summarization

A production prompt guides an AI to create a short, balanced overview of the meeting — plain English, no spin, nothing beyond what the materials support.

3

Human review

Every recap is reviewed before it is published. The goal is accuracy and fairness, not speed.

4

Published and shared

For this project, we used AI to summarize a public meeting replay and produce a short podcast-style recap that residents can listen to in about five minutes.
