The Footprint Tracker

The Costs of Campaigning

In the last contested Bedford Town Supervisor campaign (2021), the winning candidate spent $118,021 — about $45.66 for every vote received. That feels intimidating, excessive, and wasteful if you ask me. So I’m doing things differently this year.

The Whole Picture

Money Isn’t Everything

A campaign’s footprint isn’t just dollars. Mailers use paper and ink. Signs use polypropylene plastic. Cars burn fuel to place them.

AI has a footprint too. Data centers use electricity and water. That’s worth being honest about.

So this page tracks both — money and carbon, line by line, for both campaigns.

Carbon in Context

What These Numbers Look Like

The Incumbent (2021)

~10 metric tons CO2e

  • 🚗 Roughly 2 average US passenger cars driven for a full year
  • 🛣️ About 25,000 miles of driving in a typical 25-mpg car
  • ⚡ The annual electricity use of approximately 1.5 US homes
  • ⛽ About 1,100 gallons of gasoline burned

The UNCAMPAIGN (2026)

~53 kg CO2e

  • 🚗 About 130 miles of driving — roughly Bedford to Boston and back
  • ⛽ The carbon from burning about 6 gallons of gasoline
  • 🧾 Roughly the footprint of 50 Coroplast lawn signs — which this campaign isn’t producing
  • ⚡ About 2 weeks of electricity for a typical US home

One Coroplast lawn sign ≈ 2,500 Claude queries. Don’s campaign isn’t producing any signs.

Methodology

Show Your Work

The figures on this page come from public filings and published emission-factor data. Sources are cited below — the math is open to anyone who wants to check it.

The Dollars

The incumbent’s figures come from public filings with the NY State Board of Elections — 195 individual expense records across two committees (Bedford Dems 2021, ID# 186180; Friends of Ellen Calves, ID# 18354), totaling $118,021. Every record was assigned to one of six buckets. No miscellaneous category.

The UNCAMPAIGN’s $13.12 is the actual domain registration cost — the campaign’s only expenditure to date.

NY State Board of Elections — elections.ny.gov

The Carbon

Each spending bucket received an emission factor (kg CO2e per dollar), drawn from published lifecycle data and US EPA economic input-output models. All factors are conservative midpoints.

Events, Software & Ops 0.20 Catering + office + software

UNCAMPAIGN AI: ~50 queries/day × 6-month window × 10× safety factor × ~1 Wh/query × 0.39 kg CO2e/kWh ≈ 35 kg. Hosting: ~10 kg for the full election cycle on Cloudflare Pages.

The Caveats

  • All carbon figures are estimates from published emission factors, not direct measurements.
  • Inter-committee transfers ($30,738) flow to other campaigns; downstream footprint assumed similar.
  • AI footprint is genuinely uncertain — per-query energy varies across an order of magnitude by model and session type.
  • Lawn signs are technically recyclable but practically landfilled. All calculations assume single-use.
  • The 2021 race was a slate campaign covering Supervisor, Town Board, and Town Justice. Some spending served the full ticket. We use the full $118,021 without proportional adjustment, which may modestly overstate the Supervisor-only footprint.

Sources

  • Campaign finance: NY State Board of Elections — 2021 cycle filings for Bedford Dems 2021 (ID# 186180) and Friends of Ellen Calves (ID# 18354)
  • Paper / printing: Liu et al. (2018), Renewable & Sustainable Energy Reviews — meta-analysis of 45 paper-production cases; industry LCA data (Holmen, AF&PA)
  • Polypropylene / signs: Plastics-industry LCA data (~3.4 kg CO2e per kg polypropylene)
  • AI / LLM energy: OpenAI public disclosure (June 2025, ~0.34 Wh per query); Husom et al. (2025), arXiv:2505.09598 (0.42–2.5 Wh)
  • Services intensity: US EPA Environmentally-Extended Input-Output (EEIO) data
  • Grid carbon intensity: US EPA eGRID (~0.39 kg CO2e/kWh, US average)
  • Vehicle equivalents: US EPA average passenger vehicle emissions

The filings are public. The factors are cited. Anyone can check the math.
