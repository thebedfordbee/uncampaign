## Summary

Don Scott is running for **Bedford Town Supervisor** (Bedford, NY) on a self-imposed total spending cap of **$49.99** — no fundraising machine, no mailers, no robocalls, no consultants, no door knocking. Instead, the campaign builds public civic tools, uses AI openly and with human review, and publishes its spending. The core message: **Bedford deserves a choice.**

## Five Strongest Story Angles

1. **The $13.12 campaign.** A $49.99 cap published against the $118,021 the last winning campaign spent (2021). Proof: [/footprint](https://www.donforbedford.com/footprint).
2. **AI in the open.** The campaign publishes the actual prompts behind its tools and human-reviews every position. Proof: [/pages/ai](https://www.donforbedford.com/pages/ai).
3. **The one-party-town problem.** A structural argument (citing Yale's David Schleicher) that uncontested local elections are unhealthy. Proof: [/blog/the-danger-of-one-party-towns](https://www.donforbedford.com/blog/the-danger-of-one-party-towns).
4. **Public-record civic tools that actually help.** A private Voter Report Card (no vote choice shown, nothing stored) and a plain-English guide to the 762-page town code. Proof: [Voter Report Card](https://www.donforbedford.com/best-of-bedford-voter-report-card), [Town Code Guide](https://www.donforbedford.com/bedford-how-to-guide).
5. **The pre-written concession speech.** Both victory and concession speeches, published before Election Day. Proof: [/pages/odds](https://www.donforbedford.com/pages/odds).

## About Don

Bedford resident since childhood (grew up in Bedford Village, raised his family in Katonah). Former Bedford Town Councilman (5 years); former president of the Katonah-Lewisboro School Board; former Katonah Fire Commissioner; former Wetlands Commissioner. Co-Chair of the Westchester Alliance, a cross-partisan civic organization, and active with Braver Angels of New York. Runs a legal-marketing firm in Katonah.
